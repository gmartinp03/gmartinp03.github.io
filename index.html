Projects

Cyclone Rocketry

As a member of the recovery team, I worked on researching and manufacturing a parachute ejection system for the SRAD 10k Spaceport America Competition. We reworked a reefing box system to compare it to the standard two-stage ejection system. After deciding the two-stage ejection was more reliable, I helped to create a subscale model of our main parachute for wind tunnel testing. Eventually, we manufactured the full-scale parachute and successfully recovered the rocket at the competition.

SolidWorks A380

I worked with a team of other Aerospace Majors to create a scale model of an Airbus A380 in SolidWorks. I specifically worked on the fuselage, undercarriage, horizontal stabilizers, and engine/wing connectors. I also added the aircraft livery and window details while putting the final assembly together.

Lighter Than Air

Given the 90's game show theme, Baggage, my team had to create a craft capable of stable flight that was under a weight limit, met a budget, and represented our theme. The craft was constructed from balsa wood and could be attached to a large balloon for flight. We built our craft to look like a suitcase, the centerpiece in Baggage. It was capable of stable flight and movement in all directions with three propellors and a rudder.
